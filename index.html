Archemedes Constant

Value: π or ~3.1415926535

Archemedes' constant also known as pi or π is an irrational number named after and created by Archimedes of Syracuse. Archimedes was a Greek astronomer, inventor, engineer, and mathematician. Archemedes' constant is calculated by taking the circumference of any circle and dividing it by the radius. This results in an irrational value of around ~3.1415926535. Pi is essential for circle calculations.

Euler's Number

Value: e or ~2.71828

Euler's Number also refered to as e is widely regarded as one of the most important number in math. Named after and descovered by Leonhard Euler, Euler's Number is irrational and has a value of around ~2.71828. Euler's Number is found in various areas of mathematics including natural logerithms and the study of compound interest.

The Golden Ratio

Value: 1.618:1

The Golden Ratio is found in nature and is derived from the Fibbonacci Sequence(starting with 0 and 1 the addition of the last 2 numbers being the next). The golden ratio has a value of 1.618/1 and is prevalant in architecture by providing a sence of balance. Also many spirals found in nature come from the golden ratio.

Avogadro's Constant

Value: NA or 6.02214086 × 1023 mol-1

Avogadro's Constant is a also known as NA and by definition has a value of 6.02214086 × 1023 mol-1. Named after Amedeo Avogadro, Avogadro's Constant is derived from finding the number of atoms in 12 grams of carbon. It is used in various areas of science including chemistry.
